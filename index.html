The Detroit Techno Scene

I actually wanted to call this music high tech soul from the very beginning. I never wanted to call it techno. Thats what I thought it should have been called. But nobody liked it, so... ‐Derrick May

Origin

A shared deep love of music, sparked a musical group composed of Derrick May, Juan Atkins, and Kevin Saunderson. Together, they would eventually become known as the “Belleville Three” and lay down the blueprint for techno music.

The early techno records from the Detroit scene were ignored in Michigan but tracks like Rythim Is Rythim's Strings Of Life, Model 500's No UFOs and Inner City's Good Life were gaining traction in Europe's club culture.

The Father's of Techno: The Belleville Three

Politics

Detroit, a shining example of the futuristic megametroplis many can hope for was beginning to lose its lustre around the 70s. Detroit's three big car companies were moving out of the city in search of cheaper land. Those that could afford to leave the city did and those that couldn't were abondoned.

The abondoned youth, well aware of their circumstances, craved an escape. And that escape came to be techno music.

With decaying Detroit as a muse, the resulting sound of techno is a minimal, soulful melancholy.

Influences

The Electrifying Mojo, a radio DJ personality based in Detroit, played a large role in influencing the Bellview Three's music.

Charles Johnson, the man behind the radio name, is largely known to play a broad selection of music ranging from American funk music to European electronic music.

Sonically speaking, Detroit techno was the synthesis of what The Electrifying Mojo played on his radio station. The Bellview Three have credited the radio station for putting them on to artists like P-Funk, Kraftwerk, and Michael Henderson.

Kraftwerk, a European electronic/new wave band, put Germany on the map for the trio.

Legacy

In today's day and age, techno is commended as one of the most influential electronic sounds to date.

There are many derivitive subgenres including minimal techno, ambient techno, hardcore techno, industrial techno, and intelligent dance music.
